ScanVault Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Plain-language summary: ScanVault has no server, no account, and no analytics. Everything it stores lives on your device only. This page explains exactly what that means.

Camera access

ScanVault asks for camera access to scan QR codes and barcodes. Video from your camera is processed on your device, in real time, to detect and read codes. No image or video is ever saved, uploaded, or transmitted anywhere — the moment a code is decoded, the camera frame is discarded.

What's stored, and where

Your scan history and any codes you generate are saved locally on your device only — in your browser's local storage on the web version, or in secure on-device storage once this ships as a native app. None of it is sent to a server, because ScanVault doesn't have one. Uninstalling the app or clearing site data removes it completely and permanently.

No account, no tracking

ScanVault doesn't ask you to sign up, doesn't use an account system, and doesn't run analytics or tracking SDKs of any kind. There is no way for us to see what you've scanned or generated, because that information never leaves your device.

Advertising

ScanVault's free tier is supported by a single, small banner ad. As of this version, no ad network is connected yet — that will change in a future update, at which point this section will be updated to name the network and describe what data (if any) it collects. A one-time in-app purchase removes the ad entirely, on every version, forever.

Changes to this policy

If what ScanVault collects or how it's stored ever changes, this page will be updated to match before that change ships, not after.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be raised via the app's Play Store or App Store listing page.
